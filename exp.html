EXPOSÉ

.

“It's amazing that the amount of news that happens in the world every day

always just exactly fits the newspaper." - Jerry Seinfeld


The art of journalism and information is one of the most underrated and misunderstood forms of art. For once, your artwork is

judged on what you did and did not include rather than just on what you did.

 

The story for the news break will be given to the contestants on the first day of the event.

 

Rules
• Each delegation may send in one team of minimum 3 or maximum 7 members. • The news break can be a minimum of 1 and a half minutes and a maximum of
5 minutes
• Contestants will be given a news story/article with all details included and are
required to make a news segment or "exposé" deciding how to "break" the story
on their imaginary news channel.
• Contestants can use any person already present at the event for any acting or
visualising of the characters involved in the exposé.
• All footage must be original and should not have been used in any other
competition. Any video which does not abide by this rule, will be
DISQUALIFIED.
• Portrayal of high degrees of violence, profanity and any other offensive material is not allowed.
• The videos must be submitted in AVI format. If this is not possible, only WMV or
MP4 is acceptable.
• Videos must be submitted an hour before the screening starts to the category
head, details of which will be given on the day of the event.
• All transitions or graphics used must be original.

 

The video will be judged on the basis of:
• Originality
• Story
• Cinematography
• Editing

 

Points
1st = 6
2nd = 4
3rd = 3
