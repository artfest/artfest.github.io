PHOTOGRAPHY

.


“Photography to me is catching a moment which is passing, and which is true.”-Jacques-Henr Lartigue


All photographs are memento mori. To take a photograph is to participate in another person’s mortality, vulnerability and mutability.

By slicing out this moment and freezing it, all photographs testify to time’s relentless melt.

 

The topics for Photography First Rounds are:
• Silver Lining; Landscapes (Junior Category)
• Burn the Midnight Oil; night photography (Senior Category)

 

Rules:
• Each delegation may send in two teams, one for the Senior Category and
one for the Junior Category.
• There can be a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 5 members in each team.
• There will be 2 rounds of the photography competition.
• In round 1 each team will be provided with a board of 4 feet x 4 feet on
which the display of photographs can be designed to display
• The top 5 teams from both categories will continue to the second round in which contestants will be required to take photographs on campus in
allotted areas.
• Teams will have to submit their photographs in USBs before the end of the second day. The printing will be taken care of by the Art Fest team for which
contestants will be charged.
• They will then decorate a board with these photographs and the winner will be decided on the basis of these displays.
• Each team will have to display 5-10 photographs.
• The photographs must be related to the topic given and must not be less
than A4 size.
• Photographs must be completely unedited (i.e. Photoshop, etc). Also,
plagiarism will lead to disqualification.
• The participants must bring all of the equipment and materials they require for the presentation (i.e. laptops, USBs, UHU glue, double tape, accessories for decoration). They will be allotted 21/2 hours for each presentation.
• Only glue and tape are to be used to stick the pictures on the board.

 

Photographs will be judged on the basis of:
1) Content
2) Originality
3) Quality of the Images
4) Use of Light
5) Display on the Boards


Points
1st = 5
2nd= 3
3rd= 2
