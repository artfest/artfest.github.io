3D ART

.


“I recently took up ice sculpting. Last night I made an ice cube. This morning I made twelve. I was prolific.”-Mitch Hedberg


Sculpting is definitely one of the most liberating forms of art in the sense that you are allowed to create your own canvas with your medium of choice.

It allows you to express your creativity in three dimensions, making it more visual than any other form of art.

 

The criteria for 3D Art is:
• the use of mix media

 

Rules:
• Each delegation may send in one team of minimum 2 and maximum 4

members to work on a single sculpture.
• The total time for the 3D Art Competition is 8 hours split accross two days; 6 hours with a one hour break in between on the

second day of the competition (12th November 2016) and 2 hours on the third day (13th November 2016).
• References are allowed.
• Participants have the freedom to use any mix of mediums for their sculpture, e.g. clay, junk, wire, etc.
• Participants must bring all the art materials required.
• The sculpture should not depict any profanity, drug reference or other offensive material.
• There is no restriction on the size for the sculpture.
• A carpenter will be available on the premises.

 

The 3D Art will be judged on the basis of:
1) Form/Shape
2) Use of Medium
3) Originality
4) Quality of Work & Finesse


Points
1st = 5
2nd= 3
3rd= 2
