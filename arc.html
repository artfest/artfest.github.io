ARCHITECTURAL DRAWING

.


“We shape our buildings; thereafter they shape us”-Winston Churchill


Architecture helps shape the people inside a building. Buildings are proven to have the largest impact on a person's personality and demeanour.

 

The topic for Architectural Drawing:
Participants will be drawing the buildings celebrating the religious diversity that has been a part of Aitchison College

since its creation in 1886 - The Gurdwara (Junior Category) & Mandar (Senior Category)

 

Rules:
• Each delegation may send in two participants; one for the Senior Category and one for the Junior Category
• The time for the Architectural Drawing Competition is 6 hours, with an hour’s break in between.
• Make full use of the size and shape of the paper provided. (Approx. 15”x 20”)
• The drawing must be mono chromatic.
• Participants have the freedom to utilise all sorts of mediums e.g. pencil, charcoal, dry pastel etc.
• Participants must bring all the art materials required, e.g. drawing boards and mediums etc.
• The use of rulers and other mechanical instruments will not be allowed.
• Background and foreground is expected to give substance to your drawing.

 

Architectural Drawing will be judged on the basis of:
1) Drawing/Form
2) Composition
3) Personal Qualities
4) Closeness to Reality


Points
1st = 3
2nd= 2
3rd= 1
